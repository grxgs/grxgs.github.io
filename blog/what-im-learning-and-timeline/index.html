What I'm learning and its timeline
Jan 15, 2017
2 minutes read

Intro

At this point, it would be useful to layout exactly what I plan on learning. Having everything laid out and planned makes it easier to keep progressing. This timeline will be for 30 days.

I’ll primarily be focusing on learning (or relearning) the Python programming language. To accomplish this, I’ll be following “Python Crash Course” by Eric Matthes.

I chose this particular book mainly for Part II of the book which is focused on 3 different types of projects. Along with that, each chapter ends with several exercises to reinforce the lessons. For anyone curious, I’ll be using Python 3 to go through the book.

Timeline

  • Days 1 - 5: Chapters 1 - 4
  • Days 6 - 10: Chapters 5 - 8
  • Days 11 - 15: Chapters 9 - 11
  • Days 16 - 20: Chapters 12 - 14 (Project 1)
  • Days 21 - 25: Chapters 15 - 17 (Project 2)
  • Days 26 - 30: Chapters 18 - 20 (Project 3)

You can find the table of contents for “Python Crash Course” here.

For those who remember learning programming this timeline is obviously aggressive. The reason this timeline is so aggressive is because I have previous programming experience. In Python at that.

These 30 days will serve more as a reintroduction to the world of programming and Python. I’ll be posting updates every 5 days. However, you will see commits over on my Github everyday.

Until next time…
